# Operations Mid-Semester Report  

---

## Define what recurring tasks you are doing within your project group

As Project Manager, my recurring responsibilities include:

- Monitoring weekly report submissions in the Slack project channel
- Reviewing submitted reports for completeness and clarity
- Translating report content into the Weekly Operations Tracker
- Recording status across three categories:
  - Reported
  - Advisor-provided
  - Assumed
- Maintaining visibility of project progress for administrative review
- Attending weekly team meetings and tracking task direction from the advisor
- Following up with researchers when submissions or communication are missing

---

## How are you assessing your role as the project manager and its effect on the overall project?

My role is primarily operational rather than directive.

I act as a:
- coordinator of reporting flow
- interpreter of researcher activity

My impact on the project is most visible in:
- maintaining continuity despite inconsistent participation
- ensuring that project activity is still represented in the tracker
- clarifying expectations when needed (e.g., reporting process)

I am not directly influencing technical direction but am, instead, supporting visibility and structure.

---

## What challenges & successes have you observed in your group?

**Challenges:**

- One researcher has not participated in reporting, meetings, or communication
- Reporting expectations were not initially clear to all participants
- No formal enforcement mechanism exists for reporting compliance
- Communication is inconsistent and often requires follow-up

**Successes:**

- One researcher transitioned from non-submission to consistent reporting after clarification
- Weekly tracking has remained consistent despite incomplete inputs

---

## How are you collaborating with stakeholders to ensure your role is supporting and motivating the group?

Collaboration is primarily operational and communication-based:

- Clarifying expectations verbally during meetings
- Following up through Slack for missing submissions
- Maintaining a consistent tracking structure to reflect progress

Motivation is indirect. Rather than being an enforcer of behavior, I aim to:
- Reduce and and all confusion
- Make reporting expectations clearer

---

## What feedback have you received on your role solicited or unsolicited?

There has been limited explicit feedback on my role.

However, implicit feedback can be observed through:
- improved compliance after clarification
- continued lack of engagement from one participant despite outreach

These results suggests that clarity can improve participation, but does not guarantee engagement.

---

## What plans do you have for enhancing your role as the project manager with the remaining time in the semester?

For the remainder of the semester, I plan to:

- Continue reinforcing reporting expectations early and clearly
- Use the clarified reporting procedure to reduce confusion
- Maintain consistent tracking practices
- Monitor whether clearer structure improves reporting behavior
- Focus on documentation to ensure the process can be reused


---

## Please provide feedback on what support you may need in your role

Additional support that would be helpful includes:

- Clearer enforcement expectations for non-participation among student researcehers
- More formal onboarding or communication of reporting requirements
- Alignment across teams on how reporting expectations are introduced

## Weekly Reporting Workflow Clarification Initiative

### Scope of Initiative

This initiative focuses on clarifying the end-to-end weekly reporting workflow within HAAG. While a reporting structure already exists (weekly reports, Slack submission, PM tracking, and admin review) the process is currently fragmented across multiple sources such as Slack messages leading to potential missed communication and a fundamental lack of understanding of weekly reporting necessity.

The goal of this initiative is to consolidate the existing workflow into a single structured procedural reference that can be easily understood and followed by researchers without creating an entirely new system. The primary objective is to reduce confusion around expectations and improve consistency in report submission and usage.

---

### Evidence of Alignment with HAAG Goals

This initiative aligns with HAAG’s goals of improving visibility and operational consistency across research teams.

Evidence supporting this includes direct observation of inconsistent reporting behavior early on in the semester (Spring 2026). One researcher did not initially submit weekly reports but they began to submit consistently throughout the entire semester after expectations were clarified verbally. This change indicates that the issue was not workload-related but instead tied to unclear expectations.

A second researcher remained inactive throughout the semester, which further highlights the importance of having clearly defined and consistently communicated expectations. This did, however, mean that validation opportunities were limited due to team constraints. Only one researcher remained consistently active, and the computational advisor’s involvement was limited to a weekly 30-minute meeting. As a result, no additional structured feedback or iteration cycles were conducted during the semester.

Despite these limitations, the observed behavioral change following clarification supports the hypothesis that improved workflow clarity leads to improved adherence.

---

### Procedures Generated

This initiative generates a one singular procedure:

* **Weekly Reporting Workflow (Researchers)**

**Purpose:**
To provide a clear, step-by-step process for completing and submitting weekly reports, and to clarify how those reports are used within the broader HAAG tracking system.

---

## Procedure: Weekly Reporting Workflow (Researchers)

### Intended Audience

Researchers within HAAG project teams

---

### Step-by-Step Procedure

#### Step 1 — Complete Weekly Report

Each week, the researcher must complete the official weekly report template.

The report should include:

* Summary of work completed during the week
* Planned work for the following week
* Any blockers or challenges
* Proof of work (code, documentation, screenshots, etc.)
* Research component (if applicable)

---

#### Step 2 — Submit Report

* Convert the report to PDF
* Upload it to the project’s Slack channel
* Submission deadline: Friday at 5 PM ET

---

#### Step 3 — Acknowledge Submission Expectations

* Ensure awareness of submission requirements through initial Slack communication or onboarding
* Confirm that the report is visible to:

  * Project Manager (PM)
  * Computational Advisor

---

#### Step 4 — PM Tracking (Context for Researchers)

After submission:

* The PM reviews the report
* Extracts key updates and blockers
* Updates the Operations Tracker

(Researchers are not responsible for this step but should understand how their report is used within the system.)

---

#### Step 5 — Follow-Up on Blockers

If blockers are reported:

* Be prepared to discuss them in team meetings
* Provide clarification if requested
* Participate in resolution efforts as needed

---

## Integration into HAAG Workflow

This procedure integrates directly into the existing HAAG workflow without introducing new tools or processes.

* Slack remains the primary submission platform
* The existing weekly report template is retained
* The Operations Tracker continues to be maintained by the PM

The procedure formalizes how these components connect, ensuring that reporting, tracking, and review operate as a cohesive system rather than separate steps communicated informally.

---

## Researcher-Facing Access to Reporting Instructions

To ensure this procedure is effectively used, it must be surfaced directly to researchers at the point of **onboarding** and **task execution.**

Recommended placement:

* Included in the initial Slack announcement outlining weekly reporting expectations
* Linked alongside the official weekly report template
* Referenced by Project Managers during initial team meetings

Usage expectation:

* Researchers should be directed to this document before their first report submission
* The document should serve as the single reference point for:

  * submission requirements
  * report structure
  * expectations for completeness

This ensures that expectations are communicated consistently and reduces reliance on informal clarification.

---

## Enforcement and Sustainability

Adherence to this procedure is supported through existing structures:

* Weekly submission deadlines establish a consistent cadence
* PMs monitor submission compliance through the Operations Tracker
* Missing reports are identified and followed up on directly
* Behavioral reinforcement occurs through clarification and repeated use

Observed outcomes indicate that once expectations are clearly communicated, compliance improves without requiring any additional enforcement mechanisms.

---

## Realism of Implementation

This procedure is realistic because it does not introduce any additional workload beyond existing expectations. It, instead, clarifies tasks that researchers are already expected to complete and does not require new tools, training, or coordination overhead.

ALso, it accounts for limited availability of stakeholders, including:

* Researchers with varying levels of engagement
* Computational advisors with limited meeting time

As a result, the procedure can be implemented within the current HAAG structure without additional resource requirements.

---

## Iteration and Adjustments

No major modifications were made to the procedure after initial clarification.

This is due to limited opportunities for iteration:

* Only one researcher remained consistently active
* A second researcher remained inactive
* Computational advisor interaction was limited

Despite these constraints, the observed improvement in reporting behavior following clarification supports the effectiveness of the procedure in its current form.

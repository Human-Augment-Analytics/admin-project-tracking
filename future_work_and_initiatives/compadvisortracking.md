
# Computational Advisor and HAAG Admin Members Communication Workflow
###### Jaskiran Pental

## Purpose

This initiative reviews the current modes of communication and project updates between computational advisors and the HAAG Leadership team. The initiative also incorporates mechanisms to monitor the consistency and thoroughness with which computational advisors document researcher performance.

Currently, researchers submit a weekly report through which they communicate their weekly progress to their advisors. Computational advisors then conduct a HAAG administered weekly survey assessing individual researchers' progress and qualitative observations. Although this survey gives HAAG leadership a sense of how each team is progressing, it is too broad and relatively unclear how thoroughly the computational advisors are reviewing weekly reports and tracking progress/contributions from each researcher.

## Current Communication Workflow

Based on observation and documentation review, the current work flow is as follows:

1. Computational advisors meet with team on a bi-weekly basis. They lead meetings by going around and asking researchers for progress updates.
2. Through meetings and weekly reports, individual researchers document their weekly progress, blockers, and what they plan on working on the following week. 
3. Each week after report submissions and/or team meetings, computational advisors fill out the weekly advisor check-in survey, which triggers the automated Slack notifications and updates HAAG leadership. 
4. HAAG Leadership intervenes if there is an alert requiring attention of a computational advisor reaches out. 

## Observations of Current Workflow

My initial research and observations:

1. No documentation of if advisors are reading through researchers' individual reports and how thoroughly they are reviewing them. 
2. The weekly advisor check-in survey is quite broad; only three broad categories for documenting researcher contribution 
3. Weekly advisor check-in does not have section to add detailed/qualitative comments about each researcher's contribution
4. Still need to observe the frequency of communication between HAAG leadership and computational advisors 


There is a need to more closely monitor the consistency and thoroughness of computational advisors’ documentation of researcher performance. Further, there is a need to improve communication (regarding updates/needs/questions) between HAAG leadership and computational advisors that is well-organized and documented in one place.

## Computational Advisor Reporting & Accountability Framework

This initiative improves upon the current workflow and outlines a clear 

This initiative introduces a structured clarification doc that includes:

* Improved, more detailed weekly check-in survey for computational advisors
* Separate slack channel for PMs, computational advisors and HAAG leadership members involved in tracking progress
* Monthly check ins amongst the PMs, computational advisors and HAAG leadership in which PMs take meeting notes and share across all groups



---

## Working Hypothesis

If there is improved clarity with which computational advisors communicate their researchers' progress, then each research project will be more likely to stay on track and meet each team's end of semester goals. This is to be done through improving the computational advisors' workflow to stay concise yet to also outline detailed individual researcher progress. 

---

## Pilot Design

The initiative will be tested within a small cohort during a short observation window.

**Phase 1: Baseline Observation**
* Observe two weeks of how computational advisors communicate with HAAG leadership and document researchers' progress.
* Record observations.
* Interview computational advisors of at least three teams to learn about their experience with HAAG and what can be improved upon. 
* Interview HAAG leadership members (those involved with tracking, specifically) to determine how they ensure each team is on track and when they decide to step in. Will also ask if survey results are sufficient for their understanding of each project.

**Phase 2: Protocol Introduction**
* Change the Weekly check in survey in following ways:
* Include more categories than "good", "needs improvement", and "poor"
* Add a text box for computational advisors to write feedback or updates for each researcher (will be an optional field)
* Add a brief mandatory text box outlining where the research group is at in terms of progress and relative to their long term goal

* Have two to three computational advisors use this improved survey and ask them for feedback on the improved survey in comparison to old one
* After updated weekly check in has been implemented, ask the HAAG leadership if there is more clarity from advisors on how well the team is doing


---

## Measurement Criteria

Two indicators will be used to evaluate impact.

1. Qualitative results
   * Feedback from computational advisors that use the old and new survey
   * Feedback from HAAG leadership members regarding the new survey and how effective survey results are in helping their understanding of each project's progress. 
   
2. Quantitative results
   * Simple, easy to follow survey of how computational advisors and HAAG leadership feel about the current process. After protocol implementation, will resend the survey and compare the results. 

Baseline and post protocol results will be compared descriptively.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

The initiative will be considered directionally effective if:

* Computational advisors can clearly communicate if they are taking steps to reach the long-term team goals
* Computational advisors have a very clear idea of what each researcher is working on 
* HAAG leadership members feel confident that each team is on track. 
* Less ambiguity between computational advisors and HAAG leadership. 

---

## Scope Boundaries

This initiative does not:
* Modify deadlines.
* Change reporting format.
* Redesign tracking structure.

This initiative does:
* Alter specificity of weekly check-in surveys
* Changes UI of weekly check-in surveys to include more metrics

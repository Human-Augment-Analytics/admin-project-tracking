# Midterm Initiative Reflection: Milestones and Larger Themes

## Initiative Summary

This initiative introduces a simple structure for tracking project progress through:

* Milestones (short-term, concrete targets)
* Larger themes (long-term strategic focus areas)

The goal is to improve clarity, consistency, and alignment across teams.

---

## What I Completed So Far

* Defined a baseline structure for milestone and theme documentation.
* Identified core fields for milestone tracking (owner, scope, timeline, status).
* Identified core fields for theme tracking (focus area, related projects, impact).
* Created initial documentation in the repository to standardize usage.

---

## What Is Working

* The milestone/theme split is easy to understand.
* The structure is simple enough to use without extra tools.
* It supports clearer communication during weekly check-ins.

---

## Challenges Observed

* Teams may interpret milestone status labels differently.
* Updates can become inconsistent if owners are not clearly assigned.
* Theme-level progress is harder to measure than milestone completion.

---

## Midterm Learning

* Simple documentation is more likely to be adopted.
* Clear ownership is as important as clear format.
* Operational consistency needs lightweight review habits, not just templates.

---

## Adjustments for the Second Half

* Add a standard status definition guide (Not Started, In Progress, At Risk, Complete).
* Require one owner per milestone entry.
* Add a monthly theme review checkpoint.
* Keep documentation brief to reduce update burden.

---

## Expected End-of-Term Outcome

By the end of the term, this initiative should provide:

* More consistent milestone reporting
* Better visibility into cross-project priorities
* Clearer connection between weekly work and long-term goals

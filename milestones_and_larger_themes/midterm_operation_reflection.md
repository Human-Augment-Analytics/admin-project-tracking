# Midterm Operation Reflection: Milestones and Larger Themes

## Operational Need

Current operations capture weekly activity, but strategic continuity and milestone accountability are not always explicit.
This reflection focuses on improving clarity from day-to-day execution to long-term project direction.

---

## Current Operation (Observed)

* Teams report weekly updates through existing channels.
* Progress details are available, but often distributed across multiple sources.
* Long-term themes are discussed, but not always documented in a structured way.
* Status updates vary by team and are not always comparable.

---

## Operational Gaps

* No single lightweight reference connecting milestones to larger themes.
* Inconsistent milestone naming and status language across teams.
* Limited shared view of how short-term tasks support long-term goals.
* Escalation timing for at-risk work is inconsistent.

---

## Midterm Operational Insights

* Teams need a structure that adds clarity without adding major overhead.
* A minimal shared format improves cross-team readability.
* Monthly theme review is needed to keep strategy visible.
* Milestone quality improves when ownership and completion evidence are explicit.

---

## Operational Changes Proposed

* Use one standard milestone template across participating teams.
* Require common status labels and simple completion criteria.
* Link every milestone to one larger theme.
* Add a weekly PM review and a monthly theme review checkpoint.
* Add a basic escalation trigger for milestones marked At Risk.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Student Researcher | Update milestone progress and attach proof of work |
| Project Manager | Validate status quality, resolve ambiguity, escalate risks |
| Computational Advisor | Confirm milestone relevance to research direction |
| HAAG Leadership | Review theme-level progress and support resource decisions |

---

## Validation Plan (Second Half)

1. Baseline Observation (2 weeks)
* Track update completeness, status quality, and risk escalation delays.

2. Structured Rollout (3 weeks)
* Apply common template, status definitions, and owner requirements.
* Run weekly PM reviews and one monthly theme review.

3. Evaluation (1 week)
* Compare baseline and post-rollout quality metrics.
* Collect short PM/advisor feedback on usability and clarity.

---

## Midterm Metrics to Track

* Milestones with owner and due date
* Milestones with unambiguous status
* Milestones marked complete with evidence
* At-risk milestones escalated within one review cycle
* Theme records updated during monthly review

---

## Expected Operational Impact

* Better planning transparency
* Faster status interpretation
* Stronger alignment between weekly tasks and long-term objectives
* Earlier intervention on delayed or blocked work

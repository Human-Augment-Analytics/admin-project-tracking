# Operations Mid-Semester Report - Pental (Dey Lab)

---

## Define what recurring tasks you are doing within your project group

As Project Manager, my recurring responsibilities include:

- Organization of weekly meetings with researchers, computational advisors, and the faculty advisor
- Attending weekly meetings and taking notes for the team
- Ensuring researchers engage by writing and submitting weekly reports to the designated folders in our Slack channel
- Tracking individual researcher / group progress through uploading summaries of work done each week into Weekly Operations Tracker
- Following up with researchers if weekly report submissions are missing 
- If researchers miss a meeting, requesting they send a brief update in the channel day of the meeting 


---

## How are you assessing your role as the project manager and its effect on the overall project?

I treat my role as more of a organizer/coordinator to ensure every member is on the same page and so that the project can progress.
I make sure each researcher engages each week through meetings and weekly reports, and I also facilitate communication between the computational
advisors, researchers, and the faculty member. 

My role has a positive impact on the overall project because I hold the researchers accountable, which means they are expected
to continue to make some sort of progress each week. I remind researchers to submit weekly reports and have created an organized
manner in which they can do so. I have facilitated researcher-only meetings which have been productive so the researchers can talk more 
in-depth about their individual contributions and how they relate to each other's progress. Lastly, I ensure there is no miscommunication amongst the team
and complete any tasks I get from the computational advisors. 

Overall, my role has resulted in a more productive, organized environment.


---

## What challenges & successes have you observed in your group?

**Challenges:**

- There are two researchers who do not participate as much as the other researchers. One in particular has not been contributing in
  the sense that they have no submitted their weekly reports in weeks despite the fact that I have reminded them several times. 
- Meetings can be slightly awkward/unorganized at first unless I as the project manager initiate the conversation
- Initially, weekly reporting submission within Slack was messy and unorganized, which made it hard to track our progress
- Since it is a new project, the first month or so was spent on project exploration, which felt slightly off track

**Successes:**

- 4/5 researchers have become more engaged and motivated
- Weekly tracking of reports has remained consistent 
- The project is progressing and the computational advisors seem to be happy with the progress

---

## How are you collaborating with stakeholders to ensure your role is supporting and motivating the group?

I am collaborating with the researchers, computational advisors, and faculty advisor via Slack and Teams meetings. 
I also communicate with the HAAG Admin team if I have any concerns or problems I do not know how to solve so that I can get
the proper feedback and implement it. I am quite consistent about communicating via Slack with stakeholders. I also try to 
make sure the researchers stay motivated and on track by making sure expectations from the computational advisors are clearly
communicated to them. Lastly, if the researchers feel a bit disconnected, I have organized researcher only meetings so that
they can all get on the same page.

---

## What feedback have you received on your role solicited or unsolicited?

During meetings, I have had computational advisors and researchers thank me for keeping the team organized, especially since it 
is a new project that was confusing to navigate at first. 

The general flow of our team has improved as I became more confident in my role as a project manager. Weekly reports
by most researchers are submitted on a timely manner, and most researchers attend and are engaged in meetings. We even started
having separate researcher/manager only meetings, which has made each researcher's progress more clear to others. 

I could improve as a project manager since there is one researcher who is inconsistent about submitting weekly reports despite
the fact that I follow up. It might be best to try new ways to motivate this researcher.

---

## What plans do you have for enhancing your role as the project manager with the remaining time in the semester?

I plan on continuing to communicate weekly report deadlines and to remind those who have not submitted theirs in time. I will
also routinely update the weekly reporting tracker. One goal I have for enhancing my role is to summarize each of the weekly reports
for the computational advisors into one clear document so they can quickly scan the work that has been done in order to fill out the 
surveys for each researcher every week. I plan on creating an AI workflow to do so.
Overall, I want to make sure that the team stays productive and that I continue to facilitate
communication amongst all the members.

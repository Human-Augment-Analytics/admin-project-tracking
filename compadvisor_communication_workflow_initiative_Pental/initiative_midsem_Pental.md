# Initiative Mid-Semester Report - Pental
**Title:** Computational Advisor and HAAG Admin Members Communication Workflow

---

## Describe your initiative / procedure

This initiative reviews the current modes of communication and project updates between computational advisors and the HAAG Leadership team. The initiative also incorporates mechanisms to monitor the consistency and thoroughness with which computational advisors document researcher performance.

The current workflow is as follows:

1. Computational advisors and researchers meet on a biweekly or weekly basis.
2. Researchers submit weekly reports to their project Slack channel by Friday at 5PM ET.
3. Computational advisors follow a link and fill out a short survey to grade each researcher
   based on their updates/reports for the week. 
4. Survey triggers automated Slack notifications, allowing leadership to quickly identify teams requiring intervention. The three status updates are:
On track - green checkmark
Needs Improvement - yellow warning
Blocked - alert requiring attention


Although this process works, there is no documentation of if advisors are reading through researchers' weekly reports/how thoroughly they are reviewing them.
Furthermore, tThe weekly advisor check-in survey is quite broad; only three broad categories for documenting researcher contribution
Weekly advisor check-in does not have section to add detailed/qualitative comments about each researcher's contribution. 

There is a need to more closely monitor the consistency and thoroughness of computational advisors’ documentation of researcher performance. Further, there is a need to improve communication (regarding updates/needs/questions) between HAAG leadership and computational advisors that is well-organized and documented in one place.


---

## Explain the hypotheses / KPIs you have measured at this time and what is left to be measured

**Hypothesis:**

If there is improved clarity with which computational advisors communicate their researchers' progress, then each research project will be more likely to stay on track and meet each team's end of semester goals. This is to be done through improving the computational advisors' workflow to stay concise yet to also outline detailed individual researcher progress.

**KPIs:**

1. **Weekly Check-in Survey Completion Rate**
   -            Overall, want to capture the statistics with how survey completion works right now. 
   - How many computational advisors are completing the weekly surveys every week? 
   - Are they filling out the surveys for each researcher on their team?

2. **Satisfaction with current weekly check-in surveys**
   - Measured through a Google survey sent to computational advisors asking them to rate
   the current process 

**What has been observed so far:**

- So far, I have only gotten verbal feedback from the two computational advisors that lead my team, which is the AI for Mental Health Sensing group. 
They have stated that they were confused about which category out of good, needs improvement, and poor they should choose if there is a researcher
that is not participating at all. They thought the current survey was too vague and could have more answer choices.

Only qualitative observations have been collected so far but I plan to collect more observations.
